What is MeMetrics?

My name is Tyler Hirschel and I am a software engineer living in Chicago. I built MeMetrics to have a tool that could automatically gather and analyze data from various services I use and then display them using beautiful visuals to have an unique look into my life.

What data is used?

In my day to day life, I use quite a few different electronic services and I realized that many of these services could be accessed programmatically. Text messages, recruiter messages or emails, ride sharing trips, and credit card transaction are a few examples. Some of these data points can also be combined together to create a more full picture of what the trend of the data seems to indicate.

How is the data collected?

Data is collected two ways: through official apis that the service provides or by scrapping the service to retrieve the information.

On a given interval, MeMetrics will start reaching out to all the different data sources and collecting the raw data in the format provided by the service. The data gets cleaned up and normalized into the format MeMetrics needs to allow for aggregation and analysis. Finally the data saved into the database where reports can be generated.
